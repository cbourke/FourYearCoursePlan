    Help

    The CSE Four Year Course Planner application is meant to assist students in developing and following a four-year course plan for a degree in either Computer Science or Computer Engineering. This tool should be considered as an aid only and is not a substitute for regular consultations with an academic adviser. You can schedule a consultation with an academic adviser by visiting our advising page at http://cse.unl.edu/advising. You may contact us directly with questions or technical problems by sending us an email: advising@cse.unl.edu

    Creating a Course Plan

    The best way to use this tool is to let us assist you in developing an initial plan by answering the questions on the setup page. Once an initial plan has been created, you can drag-and-drop any course between semesters and the course list to the left.

    Courses – Each course can be dragged and dropped between semesters and the course list to the left. Doing so will update your schedule and credit hours. Each course also has an info icon that you can click that will generate a popup with more detailed information on each course. Be sure to save any changes that you make!

    Course List – To keep things organized, standard CSCE courses are contained in the course list on the left. You may expand/collapse and even shuffle each course group.

    Placeholder Courses – Many degree requirements are general enough that many different courses can satisfy them (ACE requirements, Natural Science requirements, etc.). The course planner defines several placeholder courses (ACE 1, NSCI 1, LANG 1, etc.) that are not real courses but serve as a placeholder for when you decide which actual courses to take.

    Course Search – UNL offers over 4,000 courses; the course list is only a small sample of Computer Science and other core courses. The course search allows you to search the entire course catalog and pull in additional courses. Simply type the course number (example: PHIL 106) or a description and several matches will automatically be generated. Click on the one you are interested in and click “Add” to pull the course in. The course will appear below the search area and you will be able to drag and drop it into your schedule.

    Adding Terms to Your Schedule

    You can modify your schedule by adding terms and even transfer courses. These options are available under the Schedule navigation option.

    New Schedule – Under this option you may also create a new schedule by either clearing out your current schedule or starting over in the setup page..

    Add Term(s) – You can add terms (Spring, Summer or Fall) under this option. Adding terms will also add intermediate spring and fall semesters as necessary.

    Add Transfer Credits – Selecting this option will create a new area that you can drag courses that you've received transfer credit for.

    Clean up – Selecting this option is a convenient way to remove terms that do not have any courses in them. Intermediate terms will remain as necessary, but all trailing, leading, and summer terms with no courses will be removed. You can always add them back in if you need to.

    Saving/Loading

    You can save your course plan and load a previously saved course plan. These actions are available in the top navigation bar under "Save/Load".

    If you are logged in, your plan will save/load from our secure database. If you are not logged in or do not have a My.UNL account, your plan will be saved/loaded from your browser’s local storage mechanism (if it is supported).

    Other Actions

    Several other options and actions can be performed. Each of the following options is available under the “Action” menu in the top navigation bar.

    Download as PDF – You can download a PDF version of your schedule which you can save for off-line viewing or print out when you meet with an academic adviser.

    Options – Under the options menu, you may set various options and settings. At this time, the options are limited to which degree (Computer Science, Computer Engineering) you are pursuing.

    Validate Plan – The applications supports some basic validation including prerequisite and credit hour checking. You can validate your plan by selecting this option. A list of errors or warnings, if any, will be displayed on the right-hand side.

    However, this application does not check for all degree requirements and should not be used to determine whether or not the plan you’ve created will satisfy your graduation requirements. You may use another tool, My Degree Audit available through MyRED to determine which requirements you’ve satisfied and which you still need to satisfy. You should also meet with an academic adviser on a regular basis. Visit our advising page for more information and to schedule an appointment.
